About Event

​On Friday May 29th, we're bringing together your fellow squiggly, spiky, dot-connecting, interdisciplinary generalists for a special Toronto Tech Week meetup!

​Join for an evening of drinks, nibbles and conversation, swapping squiggly career stories and sharing advice, experience and excitement on interesting projects, portfolio career journeys, and entrepreneurship / solopreneurship.

​This event is an official part of Toronto Tech Week :)

​In all likelihood, this room will be filled with the most fascinating and multi-faceted crowd of any event in this year's Toronto Tech Week. You wouldn't want to miss it.

​Hosted by Generalist World, the breakthrough global community that's leading the charge - and writing the book - on the generalist movement.

​Sponsored by Gamma, your new favourite free-to-use AI design partner for creating effortless presentations, websites, and more. No coding or design skills required.​​ Get 1 month free of Gamma Pro with the code GENERALIST

​Organized by Dave Mence, active advocate for the generalist movement, long-time Generalist World community member, organizer of the Generalist World Toronto Community and Program Ops Manager at Notion (a decidedly generalist role).

​What is a generalist? Professionals who apply their skills across multiple domains, connect disparate ideas, or serve as "translators" between different teams and disciplines. If you've ever felt your career doesn't fit in a single box, this event is for you.

​What to expect:

  • ​Connect with fellow generalists

  • ​Celebrate the power of diverse skillsets and squiggly careers

  • ​Swap squiggly career stories and practical advice

​Venue:

​This event is being hosted downtown on King St. W near Bathurst St., with easy TTC access and ample parking options. The location will be shared with each of you in your registration confirmations :)

​Toronto Tech Week:

​This event is an official part of Toronto Tech Week 2026 - a citywide celebration of the people building what’s next. From May 25–29, 2026, founders, investors, and builders come together for hundreds of community-led events across Toronto, connecting tens of thousands of people around Canadian tech | torontotechweek.com
