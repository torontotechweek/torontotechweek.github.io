About Event

​From idea → prototype in 1 day. No more waiting.

​Most people don’t lack ideas.
They lack the space, structure, and support to actually start.

​During Toronto Tech Week, we’re changing that.

​ Why This Event Exists

​We’ve seen this pattern across thousands of students, professionals, and founders:

  • ​“I’ve been thinking about this idea for months…”

  • ​“I want to build something but don’t know where to start…”

  • ​“I’m not technical, so I don’t think I can…”

​The reality today?

​👉 AI has changed the game.
👉 The barrier to building is lower than ever.
👉 But execution is still the biggest gap.

​This event is designed to close that gap.

​Not through another panel.
Not through theory.

​But by getting you to actually build something, in the room.

​What This Experience Is

​The AI Build Lab is a 5–6 hour guided sprint + workshop + showcase designed to help you go from:

​👉 idea → problem clarity → MVP → live demo

​All in one day.

​You’ll work in curated teams (with both technical + non-technical participants), supported by mentors, and move through a structured build process used by real product teams.

​As outlined in the workshop structure, each team is supported by a dedicated mentor and follows a clear build framework focused on defining the user, problem, MVP, and execution path .

​👀 Who This Is For

​This is intentionally designed to be inclusive of all skill levels:

  • ​Developers & engineers

  • ​Product managers, designers, operators

  • ​Students & early-career professionals

  • ​Newcomers exploring opportunities in tech

  • ​Non-technical individuals curious about AI

  • ​Aspiring founders & side-project builders

​👉 You do NOT need:

  • ​a full idea

  • ​a technical background

  • ​prior experience building

​Just curiosity + willingness to try.

​What You’ll Actually Do

​This isn’t a sit-and-listen event.

​You will:

  • ​Learn how AI tools can be used to build real products

  • ​Understand how to think like a product builder

  • ​Work in a team to define a real problem

  • ​Scope a realistic MVP (not overbuilt ideas)

  • ​Build a working prototype using AI tools

  • ​Get feedback from mentors

  • ​Share your work with the community

​What You’ll Leave With

​By the end of the day, you won’t just have “learned something.”

​You’ll have:

  • ​✅ A real project or prototype

  • ​✅ Hands-on experience using AI tools

  • ​✅ A portfolio-ready build (huge for job seekers)

  • ​✅ New collaborators, mentors, and connections

  • ​✅ A repeatable framework to keep building

​For many, this becomes:

  • ​your first project

  • ​your first proof of work

  • ​or the start of something much bigger

​High-Level Agenda

​🟢 11:00 – 11:30 | Arrival + Networking

​🟢 11:30 – 12:00 | Welcome + Introduction

​🟢 12:00 – 1:30 | Skill-Leveling + Live Demo

​🟢 1:30 – 2:00 | Team Formation + Scoping

​🟢 2:00 – 5:30 | 🚀 Build Sprint (With real-time mentor support throughout)

​🟢 5:30 – 6:00 | Closing + Networking
