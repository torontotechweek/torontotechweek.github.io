About Event

​Three moderated panels, startup presentations, and a networking reception exploring how AI adoption is entering Canada's construction sector.

​The room brings together general contractors, engineering consultancies, construction law firms, professional regulators, labour organizations, safety bodies, AI research institutes, construction technology founders, government agencies, and investment firms.

​The panels are expected to explore themes such as:

​Panel 1: Workforce Readiness

  • ​Education and training adapting for AI in construction

  • ​AI-enabled devices and wearables creating friction with existing safety culture

  • ​Labour relations evolving alongside new tools and monitoring

  • ​AI adoption and women's participation in the industry

​Panelists include trades bodies, workforce technology founders, labour organizations, educators, and safety bodies.

​Panel 2: Integration and Implementation

  • ​AI adoption differing by organization size and type

  • ​Integration across disciplines and delivery models

  • ​What adoption looks like from the perspective of firms building the tools

  • ​What AI research says is achievable today versus what is not

​Panelists include general contractors, engineering consultancies, enterprise technology leaders, construction technology founders, and AI research institutes.

​Panel 3: Standards and Governance

  • ​Contract language adapting for AI in project delivery

  • ​Professional practice guidelines and regulatory frameworks evolving

  • ​Surety and bonding evaluating AI-powered contractors

  • ​Standards for governing AI's reasoning process

​Panelists include construction litigators, construction lawyers, professional regulators, insurers, and project
