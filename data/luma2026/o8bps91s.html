About Event

​Most Tech Week events start with coffee. This one starts at 25 km/h.

​Watts of Innovation is a 20–25km road ride for experienced cyclists in tech. This is not a casual ride. Expect a steady pace, tight group riding, and a focused route.

​We start early so you can ride hard and still make it back for Tech Week events.

​The route heads west along the waterfront, into High Park for ~1 hour of laps and optional climbs, then finishes with coffee.

​This is intentionally a niche ride for people who take both building and riding seriously.

​Hosted by Unified.to, Forum Ventures, and Mobile Capital Network.

​Schedule

  • ​6:30 AM — Depart Princes Gates (primary start)

  • ​~6:45 AM — Pickup at Colborne Lodge & Lakeshore

  • ​6:45–7:45 AM — High Park laps + optional climbs

  • ​~8:15–8:30 AM — Coffee at Cherry Bomb

​Ride Details

  • ​Distance: ~20–25 km

  • ​Pace: 25–27 km/h average

  • ​Format: Group ride + High Park laps

​This is not a beginner or casual ride.

​Route

  • ​Martin Goodman Trail (westbound)

  • ​High Park loops (optional Ellis climb)

  • ​Exit via High Park Blvd → Roncesvalles

  • ​Finish at Cherry Bomb

​Safety

  • ​Helmets required

  • ​Call out hazards (holes, bumps, debris)

  • ​Obey all traffic signals and stop signs

  • ​Hold your line and ride predictably

​Requirements

  • ​Road bike required

  • ​No city bikes or rentals

  • ​Comfortable riding in a group at pace

​Toronto Tech Week is a citywide celebration of the people building what’s next. From May 25–29, 2026, founders, investors, and builders come together for hundreds of community-led events across Toronto, connecting tens of thousands of people around Canadian tech. Torontotechweek.com
