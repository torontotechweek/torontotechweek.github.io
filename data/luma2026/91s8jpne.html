About Event

​What does the future of technology really look like—and who’s shaping it?

​Join us during Toronto Tech Week for a powerful panel featuring women building and leading in emerging technologies. From cybersecurity to physical security and quantum innovation, we’ll explore how these fields are evolving—and what it means for the next generation of entrepreneurs.

​This is more than a panel—it’s a space to connect, exchange ideas, and build meaningful relationships with people who are shaping the future.

​What to expect:
• Insightful panel discussion with women in cybersecurity, physical security, AI, and quantum tech
• Real conversations about innovation, opportunities, and challenges
• High-value networking with entrepreneurs, founders, and tech enthusiasts

​Who should attend
Women and allies, entrepreneurs, aspiring founders, professionals, and anyone curious about the future of tech and innovation.

​Additional Information
Light food will be served.

​🎤 Speakers

​🛡️ Physical Security Leader

​Sylienne Cardichon is the founder and CEO of SELEST Security, a Toronto-based company redefining private security through innovation and technology. With a background spanning private security, investigations, security management, and real estate investment, she brings a strategic and hands-on approach to modern security challenges. Her work focuses on building safer, more efficient environments through operational excellence, analytical thinking, and client-centered solutions, while continuously exploring the intersection of security, innovation, and business growth.

​⚛️ Quantum Technology Specialist

​Gharsa Amin is a Canadian entrepreneur, software engineer, and Founder & CEO of Kavodax — Canada's first stablecoin-powered cross-border payments platform, now live across 50+ countries. With a Master's in Public Administration specializing in financial crime management and experience across seven startups in Canada, the US, and Asia, she brings rare technical depth and a compliance-first mindset to global payments. Gharsa has worked with tier-one banks and Canada's largest exchanges, and today leads a female-led team redefining how money moves across borders. Named one of Spark Centre's Top 10 Women to Watch in 2026 and a national pitch competition champion, she is building the financial infrastructure the world runs on next — in five languages, and without taking no for an answer.

​Website: www.kavodax.com
