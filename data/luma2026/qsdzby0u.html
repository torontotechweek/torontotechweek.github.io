About Event

​Toronto's builder community, together.

​Toronto is full of builders - artists, engineers, designers, founders, tinkerers...quietly working on things they care about.

​Meraki exists to bring those people into the same room.

​To celebrate an incredible year of building, we're hosting our annual Symposium and Demo Day during Toronto Tech Week - an evening where members of the community get to formally present the projects they've been pouring themselves into. It's our favourite night of the year.

​Whether you're a builder, an investor, an operator, or just someone who loves being around people making things - you belong here :)

UPDATE: DEMOER INTEREST FORM NOW OPEN: https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfxsL5vne_s9OQoUfbDe3u5vtJmL1okvojQozZKW2EshuLprg/viewform

​What’s happening:

​A few members of the Meraki UofT community will be demoing and sharing their passion projects. You'll see small glimpses into what people are building, learning, and experimenting with right now.

​You can expect:

  • ​Short demos from Meraki community members

  • ​Casual conversations around art, tech, and creative work

  • ​Food (yes, there will be food)

  • ​Space to meet people and trade ideas

​Come curious! Come ready to talk about what you’re working on or what you want to work on.

​A Note on the Meraki mindset

​Meraki is built on the idea that creative work is better when it’s shared.

​When you come, you’re not just an attendee, you’re a part of the space. We ask everyone to:

  • ​Be welcoming to newcomers

  • ​Ask thoughtful questions

  • ​Share generously, even if your work feels “unfinished”

  • ​Help make the room feel warm, curious, and alive

​If you see someone standing alone...go say hi & make a new friend.

​What is Meraki UofT?

​Meraki UofT is a student-led builder community at the University of Toronto.

(we're friends of Socratica!!)

​We bring together people across disciplines; engineering, art, design, business, research, and beyond! People who care about making things and learning in public.

​If you like building, experimenting, or exploring new ideas, Meraki is for you.

​Filming & media notice

​This event will be photographed and filmed for Meraki UofT’s (and sponsors) promotional and archival use (social media, website, and future event materials).

​By attending, you consent to being recorded. If you have any concerns or would prefer not to be filmed, please let a Meraki organizer know... we’re happy to accommodate.
