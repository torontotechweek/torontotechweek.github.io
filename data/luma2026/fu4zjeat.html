​FAQs

​What is PitchTank?

​PitchTank is a pitch competition reimagined as a live stock market. Founders pitch on stage and their startup go public on PitchTank platform. The audience trades them in real time using $1M virtual portfolios — turning every pitch into a live, crowd-priced evaluation. The founder with the highest market cap wins. The audience traders with the sharpest portfolios top the leaderboard.

​Is this really for serious investors?

​Yes. PitchTank is built around the actual question every investor asks: which of these founders would I back? The format forces you to make and defend that call in real time, against a room of people doing the same. If you've ever wanted to stress-test your investing instincts in a live environment, this is it.

​Is this an official Toronto Tech Week event?

​Yes. PitchTank x TTW is part of Toronto Tech Week 2026, co-hosted with AGIV, Amiral VC, and StartupFuel.

​Do I need investing experience to trade?

​No. The app is built for first-time and experienced traders alike. But if you do come in with an investor lens, the format rewards it — sharper evaluation usually means a sharper portfolio.

​How does the trading actually work?

​You'll get $1M in virtual capital when you check in. As each founder pitches, you can buy, sell, or hold their stock through the PitchTank app. Prices move based on what the room is doing — a live, crowd-sourced read on each founder. Your portfolio value at the end of the night determines your spot on the leaderboard.

​What do I need to bring?

​A smartphone with the PitchTank app installed. We'll send setup instructions after you register. Bring it charged — you'll be on it all night.

​How are founders selected to pitch?

​Founders apply for a pitch slot. We curate the lineup based on stage, traction, pitch readiness, and investability. Expect a curated roster of companies actively raising or about to raise. Apply through our website or grab a founder ticket to be considered.

​Are the founders actively raising?

​Most are. We curate for founders who are at or near a raise, so the room is evaluating real, live opportunities — not hypotheticals.

​Can I just come to watch?

​You can, but you'll be the only one not playing. Every audience ticket comes with a $1M portfolio and a spot on the leaderboard. There's no "spectator mode" — and frankly, the format only works if you're in.

​What's the food situation?

​Food spreads with vegetarian options. Refreshments flowing all night.

​When do doors open?

​30 minutes before the first pitch. Come early to grab food, set up your portfolio, and find your spot on the floor.
