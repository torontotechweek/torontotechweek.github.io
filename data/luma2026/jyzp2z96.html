About Event

​Most AI events are about tools.

​This one is about leadership.

​The question is no longer whether your team uses AI. They already do. The leadership question is whether your company has a clear operating model for AI: what it can access, where it can take action, who approves the work, and who owns the outcomes.

​The Agentic Boardroom brings a curated group of CEOs, founders, operators, investors, and AI leaders together during Toronto Tech Week for one focused evening at Harbour Sixty.

​We will open with a keynote from Aaqib Azeem, Global AI and ML Partner Lead at Google, on where enterprise AI is heading. From there, the conversation moves into practical leadership questions: adoption, governance, workflow ownership, risk, and the operating muscle required to turn AI from scattered experimentation into real company leverage.

​Then the room goes to work.

​Guests will be invited into the conversation through live Q&A, practitioner discussion, and facilitated boardroom roundtables.

​This is a private working room for leaders being asked to shape:
- Where AI can safely take action inside a real company
- What should still require human judgment
- Who owns AI when it moves from experiment to operating system
- What a Chief Agent Officer, or equivalent AI owner, needs to control
- What Canadian AI leadership should look like as agentic systems enter the business mainstream

​The evening is designed to give guests a clearer map of the agentic shift, a sharper view of where their organization sits, and a seat inside a founding conversation that should continue beyond the room.

​Toronto Tech Week is a citywide celebration of the people building what’s next. From May 25–29, 2026, founders, investors, and builders come together for hundreds of community-led events across Toronto, connecting tens of thousands of people around Canadian tech. Torontotechweek.com
