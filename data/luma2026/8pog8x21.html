About Event

​If you’re planning to raise your first round in the next 3–6 months, this session is designed to get you investor-ready — by working directly on your company.

​The Investor Readiness Bootcamp 2026 is a focused, one-day, in-person working session for pre-seed tech founders who already have an MVP or early traction and need clarity on how to position their company for investors.

​This is not a lecture-based program.

​Throughout the day, founders work directly on their own fundraising strategy using their laptops and a dedicated board space. The session is designed to help you structure decisions, refine your positioning, and identify risks before entering investor conversations.

​WHAT YOU WILL WORK ON

​By the end of the day, you will have:

​• A structured fundraising strategy tailored to your company
• A refined investor narrative aligned with how investors think
• Clear decisions on valuation, structure, and investor targeting
• Identified key risks and gaps that could impact your raise
• A clearer understanding of how investors evaluate opportunities and make decisions

​HOW THE DAY WORKS

​This is a working session — not a lecture.

​Each block is designed for you to actively work on your company using your laptop and a dedicated board space.

​IMPORTANT

​You will be working on your own company throughout the entire session — this is not a passive learning experience.

​PRICING

​The registration fee is per company (up to 2 founders) so teams can align on strategy together.

​Thanks to the support of Global Startups, GSA Ventures, and Dentons, approximately 90% of the program cost is covered.

​WHO THIS IS FOR

​• Founders with an MVP or early traction
• Planning to raise capital in the next 3–6 months
• Looking for clarity before approaching investors
• Wanting to avoid common fundraising mistakes

​AGENDA

​09:00 – 10:30 | Structuring your raise

​Define your funding strategy, investor profile, and timing.

​Work through how investors will evaluate your company, including cap table implications and future rounds.

​10:30 – 10:45 | Break

​10:45 – 12:00 | Building investor materials

​Work directly on your pitch and positioning.

​Clarify how to communicate your business clearly, concisely, and in a way that aligns with investor expectations.

​12:00 – 01:00 | Lunch + Legal perspective (Dentons)

​Understand key legal decisions that impact your raise — applied directly to your company.

​01:00 – 03:00 | Financial and deal structure

​Work through valuation, funding instruments (SAFE, equity, convertible notes), and deal terms — applied to your case.

​03:00 – 04:00 | Strategy review & feedback

​Review your fundraising approach, identify gaps, and refine your next steps before engaging investors.
